My Reflections

Documenting my journey in web development, design, and learning how to drive. This blog captures my reflections, progress, challenges, and successes while building my portfolio website.

Week 1

February 17, 2025

In the first week, I focused on understanding semantic markup. I learned how HTML elements can provide contextual meaning to browsers and assistive technologies. This foundational knowledge helped me structure my content in a way that improves accessibility and SEO. My main takeaway was that meaningful markup contributes to a website’s usability and discoverability.

I also set up my repository on GitHub, following the naming conventions discussed in lectures. This step ensured that my project is well-organized and easy to access online.

Week 2

February 24, 2025

This week, I focused on structuring my content in a modular way. I organized the project folders to reflect the hierarchical information structure, making it easier to manage and scale. Additionally, I explored metadata and how it can guide browsers to interpret content efficiently. This involved adding meaningful metadata tags to each page, such as author, description, and keywords.

Another key focus was incorporating microformats to make my blog posts searchable and properly indexed by search engines. I chose the h-entry microformat, which ensures that each post is well-defined and easy to reference.

Week 3

March 03, 2025

This week, I focused on enhancing accessibility by using semantic HTML elements correctly and adding ARIA attributes where necessary. I also began drafting my wireframes for the design section, making sure they align with the goal of a clear, road-themed learning experience. The challenge was balancing thematic creativity with practical accessibility.

Week 4

March 10, 2025

This week was dedicated to reflection on my progress and overcoming challenges related to maintaining semantic integrity while pursuing creative presentation. I realized that while thematic elements are important, they should not compromise the website’s usability or clarity.

To address this, I refined my design plan, ensuring that thematic elements like road sign graphics are complemented by clear text and descriptive headings. Additionally, I documented my progress and included reflections on what I learned from balancing aesthetics with functionality.
